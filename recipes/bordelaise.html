BORDELAISE

A classic French sauce made with red wine, veal demiglace, suateed shallots, butter, and bone marrow. At approximately 500 callories per Oz, it's not for the diet concious, but irresistable nonetheless. This sauce perfectly addorns lean red meats that need a little extra fat to really make them shine. A perfect accompanyment for Filet Mignon, Hanger Steak, or Venison.

Tools Required

  • Small Roasing Pan
  • Small Sauce Pan
  • Wire Whisk
  • Wire Strainer
  • Gravey Boat

Ingredients

  • One medium Shallot, Sliced thinly.
  • One large Marrow Bone, Aproximately 24 Oz.
  • 2 Tablespoons Unsalted Butter.
  • 1 Cup Dry Red Wine, (do not use cooking wine).
  • 1/4 cup or 4 Oz. (by weight) Veal Demiglace, thawed.

Directions

  1. In a roasting pan, roast marrow bones in oven set to 475°F for 30 minutes
  2. While marrow bones are roasting, melt butter in sauce pan and saute sliced
    shallots until brown and semi-translucent.
  3. Add red wine and simmer on med-low heat, stirring occationally until reduced
    to a syrup.
  4. Add veal demiglace and reduce further, approximately 50%.
  5. Add roasted bone marrow and any rendered fat in roasting pan, stir, untill
    marrow is disolved. strain into gravey boat and serve. If not ready to serve
    immediately, strain into a vaccume thermous to keep warm for up to 30 minutes.
